Privacy Policy

Principles of personal data processing and protection in the Society of Psychological Counselors.

The Society of Psychological Counselors (TDP) is committed to protecting the privacy of its members and complying with applicable data protection laws, in accordance with Regulation (EU) 2016/679 of the European Parliament and of the Council of 27 April 2016 on the protection of natural persons with regard to the processing of personal data and on the free movement of such data (GDPR) and the Act of 10 May 2018 on the Protection of Personal Data (Journal of Laws of 2018, item 1000).

Scope and Purposes of Data Processing

TDP processes the personal data of its members for the following purposes:

  • Member Registration: Processing personal data for registration and membership management.
  • Education and Training: Organization and management of courses, training, workshops, and supervision.
  • Communication: Sending information related to TDP activities, including news, invitations to events, information about training, and other forms of communication.
  • CPD Documentation: Collection and storage of documentation regarding Continuous Professional Development (CPD).
  • Legal Requirements: Processing data to fulfill legal obligations arising from personal data protection regulations.

Types of Processed Data

TDP processes the following personal data:

  • First and last name
  • Practitioner number
  • Email address
  • Phone number
  • Residential address
  • Data regarding education and professional qualifications
  • Information regarding participation in training, courses, and workshops
  • Data regarding supervision and CPD
  • Data regarding language levels
  • Other necessary data required for the implementation of statutory activities occurring during membership.

Rights of Data Subjects

TDP members have the right to:

  • Access to Data: Obtain information about the processing of their personal data and access to this data.
  • Rectification of Data: Request rectification of incorrect or incomplete personal data.
  • Deletion of Data: Request deletion of their personal data in cases specified by law.
  • Restriction of Processing: Request restriction of data processing in specific cases.
  • Data Portability: Receive their personal data in a structured, commonly used format and transfer this data to another controller.
  • Objection: Object to the processing of personal data in specific cases.

Data Security

TDP applies appropriate technical and organizational measures to protect personal data against unauthorized access, loss, destruction, or damage. Security audits and employee training on personal data protection are conducted regularly.

Data Sharing

TDP does not share its members' personal data with third parties, except when required by law or based on the member's consent. Data may be shared with TDP partners solely for purposes related to the implementation of the Society's statutory tasks, such as organizing training and workshops.

Guidelines for Counselors

The Society of Psychological Counselors (TDP) obliges its members to adhere to the highest standards of protection of clients' personal data.

Protection of Clients' Personal Data

  • GDPR Compliance: Every counselor must act in accordance with GDPR and the Personal Data Protection Act.
  • Consents: Obtaining written consent from clients for processing their data.
  • Security: Applying measures to protect against unauthorized access.
  • Confidentiality: Maintaining confidentiality during data processing.
  • Minimization: Processing only necessary data.
