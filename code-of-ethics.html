Counselor Code of Ethics

The foundation of professionalism and trust in the Society of Psychological Counselors.

Introduction

The Counselor Code of Ethics aims to establish clear and consistent ethical standards that will guide the conduct of all members of the Society of Psychological Counselors (TDP). It is intended to ensure the protection of clients' rights and dignity and to promote the highest standards of professional practice. The Code applies to all TDP members, regardless of their level of qualification or specialization, and is binding in clinical work, research, education, and supervision.

General Principles

Fundamental Ethical Principles

  • Respect: Treating all clients with dignity and respect, supporting their right to make their own decisions.
  • Integrity: Transparently informing clients about all aspects of therapy, including goals, methods, potential benefits, and risks.
  • Responsibility: Being responsible for one's professional actions and their consequences, commitment to continuous professional development.
  • Justice: Striving for equal treatment of all clients, ensuring availability and fairness of services.
  • Concern for Client's Welfare: Placing the client's welfare first, protecting privacy and confidentiality of information obtained during therapy.

Professional Duties of a Counselor

  • Professionalism: Maintaining the highest standards of professionalism, avoiding actions that could damage reputation or credibility.
  • Professional Development: Continuous improvement of skills and knowledge through participation in training, courses, and other forms of continuing education.
  • Professional Supervision: Regular participation in supervision sessions (individual, group, or peer), in accordance with TDP guidelines.
  • Documentation: Maintaining accurate documentation of all therapeutic sessions, ensuring security and confidentiality of stored information.

Ethical Standards

Confidentiality and Privacy Protection

  • Maintaining Confidentiality: All information obtained from the client must be treated as confidential unless the client has given written consent for its disclosure.
  • Legal Exceptions: Information may be disclosed without the client's consent only in situations specified by law, such as a threat to the life of the client or other persons.
  • Data Storage: Therapeutic documentation must be stored securely, protected against unauthorized access and destruction.

Consent to Participate in Therapy

  • Informed Consent: The counselor must obtain the client's written consent before starting therapy, providing full information about the therapeutic process.
  • Right to Withdraw Consent: The client has the right to withdraw consent to therapy at any time without giving a reason.

Professional Relationships and Boundaries

  • Professionalism: Maintaining professional relationships with clients, avoiding conflicts of interest and abuse of trust.
  • Professional Boundaries: Avoiding any form of personal relationships with clients that could violate the professional therapeutic relationship.
  • Avoiding Exploitation: Counselors may not use the professional relationship for personal gain, whether financial or emotional.

Professional Practice

Record Keeping

Documentation must be maintained accurately, fully reflecting the course of therapeutic sessions, diagnoses, therapeutic plans, and client progress. All records must be treated as confidential.

Client Relations

  • Building Trust: Establishing relationships with clients based on trust, honesty, and respect.
  • Communication: Using open and clear communication.
  • Impartiality: Avoiding bias and treating every client with equal care and respect.

Resolving Conflicts of Interest

Counselors must be aware of potential conflicts of interest and avoid situations that may lead to them. If a conflict arises, it should be reported and discussed with the client.

Research Ethics

Conducting research with the highest integrity and honesty. Participation in research must be fully voluntary, and participants must give informed consent.

Procedures for Code Violations

Every member has a duty to report any violations of the Code of Ethics. Reports can be made by contacting the ethics committee available on the Society's official website.

Sanctions may include a warning, suspension of membership, revocation of license, or referral of the case to appropriate legal authorities.
