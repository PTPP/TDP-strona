Registration Standards

Code of Ethics

Members must adhere to the TDP Code of Ethics, demonstrating the highest ethical standards and professional conduct.

Read full Code of Ethics →

Clean Criminal Record

A current criminal record certificate is required (not older than 3 months before the membership renewal date).

Development and Supervision

Key elements of maintaining membership.

Supervision (10h / year)

Individual Supervision

One-on-one work with a qualified supervisor.

Group Supervision

Conducted by a supervisor for a group of 2-6 participants.

Peer Supervision

For practitioners min. level II. Mutual exchange in a group of up to 6 people (protocol required).

Continuous Professional Development (CPD)

CPD-A (Active) - 50 pts (10h)

Training, workshops, conferences, live webinars (interaction).

CPD-B (Passive) - 50 pts (10h)

Reading literature, watching recordings, own research, publications.

Reporting:

CPD documentation must be submitted via the Counselor Panel by the end of February for the previous year.

Membership Policies

  • Regular payment of fees.
  • Completion and reporting of CPD hours (A and B).
  • Completion and reporting of supervision hours.
  • Adherence to the Code of Ethics.
  • Current criminal record certificate.

Members may apply for leave from the registry for personal or professional reasons. The procedure for obtaining leave and the conditions for returning to active registration are strictly defined in the regulations.

Members have the right to appeal decisions regarding their registration, promotion, or ethical sanctions. The appeal procedure ensures a fair and impartial consideration of the case.
